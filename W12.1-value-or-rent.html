Debrief: Why this question matters

The hardest part of the Apple/Epic case is that both sides are partly right. Apple does provide real infrastructure: security reviews, payment rails, global distribution, and a trusted brand. Those are genuine services. But Apple is also the only way onto the iPhone — and that monopoly position means it can charge whatever it wants for those services. The 30% fee isn't just a price; it's a gatekeeping tax. The real question isn't whether Apple provides value. It's whether Apple's control over the channel gives it the power to extract more value than it creates — and whether that's good for the ecosystem, or just good for Apple.

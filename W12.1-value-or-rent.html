Three Buckets

✓ Channel Value
Apple is genuinely making the channel better — providing infrastructure, trust, or reach that developers and consumers couldn't access on their own.
⛔ Channel Control
Apple is using its gatekeeper position to restrict alternatives, extract fees, or set terms that benefit Apple at the expense of other channel members.
⚡ Channel Conflict
This policy directly triggers conflict between Apple and other channel members — developers, regulators, or competitors who push back on Apple's terms.

Debrief: The Channel Captain Problem

The hardest part of the Apple/Epic case is that Apple is both the channel captain and a competing seller in the channel it runs. As channel captain, Apple designs the rules — security review, commission rates, payment processing. Those rules create real value. But Apple also uses that same authority to block competitors, extract fees with no market alternative, and tilt visibility toward its own apps. The question isn't whether Apple provides value. It's whether Apple's monopoly position over the channel gives it the power to extract more than it creates — and whether regulators, developers, or the market can do anything about it.

How This Works
Yesterday you tore apart a real campaign. Today you build one. Same Netflix, same only-one-Action-matters rule: someone has to actually watch the show.

The brief from Netflix

The show: Stranger Things: Hawkins Class of '86 — a spin-off series following a new group of Hawkins High students one year after the events of the final season. 8 episodes. All drop on a Friday in October 2026.

The target: fans of the original series (16–35) who are at real risk of not coming back for a spin-off they didn't ask for.

The Action, again: open Netflix and watch episode 1. That's it. If they don't watch the pilot, none of this mattered.

Your six tools — reference card

These are the six tools in the IMC toolbox from Chapter 17. You'll each take one.

Advertising
Paid, non-personal, broad reach. TV spots, billboards, banner ads, pre-roll.
Public Relations
Earned media. Press coverage, cast interviews, stunts that get reporters to write about you.
Sales Promotion
Short-term incentives. Free-trial codes, bundle deals, co-promo with a retailer.
Personal Selling
Human-to-human. Influencer partnerships, campus reps, cast-hosted watch parties.
Direct Marketing
One-to-one, targeted. Email, push notifications, personalized in-app prompts.
Digital / Social
Owned + organic. TikTok, Instagram, YouTube shorts, community, memes.

The rules

One tool per student, no duplicates within your group.
Talk first. Agree who's taking what before anyone locks in. If your group has 3 people, pick any 3 tools. If your group has 5, pick any 5. You don't have to cover all six.

Step 1 (~8 min): on your own, build a tactic card for your tool — headline, execution, target AIDA stage, and how Netflix would know it worked.

Step 2 (~10 min): your group reads all the cards, picks the 3 strongest, drags them into order (top of funnel → Action), and writes the Big Idea + Handoff story + One Risk. You each enter the group's plan on your own device.

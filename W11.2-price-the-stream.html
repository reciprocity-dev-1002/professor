Three Pricing Methods, One Decision

Your job: help StreamLocal pick a price for their local streaming service.

There are three ways to approach pricing — from least strategic to most. You'll apply all three, then decide which one actually belongs in the board deck.

The Strategic Progression
ROUND 1
Cost-Plus — least strategic. Start with your costs, add a markup.
ROUND 2
Competitive — market-aware. Anchor your price against what competitors charge.
ROUND 3
Value-Based — most strategic. Price what customers believe it's worth.

After each round you'll stop and compare with your group. At the end, vote on which method to take to the board.

Round 1: Cost-Plus Pricing

Method Definition

Start with your costs. Add a markup percentage. The price covers costs and delivers a predictable profit.

Price = Variable Cost + (Fixed Cost ÷ Subscribers ÷ 12) + Markup

Choose a markup, see your price update in real time, and explain your choice. Then compare with your group.

Round 2: Competitive Pricing

Method Definition

Look at what competitors charge. Set your price based on where you want to sit in the market — above, below, or alongside them.

Your Price = Competitor Price ± Positioning Adjustment

Watch out: This method is market-aware but ignores your own costs and the unique value you create.

What the Market Looks Like

Streaming Competitors

  • Netflix (w/ ads): $6.99/mo — huge library, zero local content
  • Hulu (w/ ads): $7.99/mo — national shows, minimal local
  • ESPN+: $10.99/mo — national sports only, no local teams
  • FuboTV (local sports tier): $17.99/mo — has local, but packed with extras most don't want

The Gap StreamLocal Can Own

  • ESPN+ has sports — but not your local teams
  • FuboTV has local — but charges $18 for a bundle you don't need
  • The unserved sweet spot: Local sports + local news, $9–$15/mo

Pick which competitor you're anchoring against, set your price, and explain your positioning. Then compare with your group.

Round 3: Value-Based Pricing

Method Definition

Ignore your costs. Ignore competitors. Price to what customers believe StreamLocal is worth to them. The most strategic method — and the hardest to execute.

Here's the trade-off: a higher price captures fewer subscribers, but more revenue per subscriber. You need to pick a target segment — then defend it.

WTP Segments (of 50,000 target subscribers)

  • $5–8/mo — 60% of market → ~30,000 subscribers
  • $9–12/mo — 25% of market → ~12,500 subscribers
  • $13–16/mo — 10% of market → ~5,000 subscribers
  • $17+/mo — 5% of market → ~2,500 subscribers

What They Value Most

  • Local sports: 78% say essential
  • Local news: 52% say essential
  • On-demand library: 41% say essential
  • Hint: the segments willing to pay more tend to care most about live local sports.

Week 14 · Exercise 2 · The Brief
The Client: Iron Ridge Gym
A local independent gym competing against Planet Fitness on price, and against boutique studios on vibe. They can't win on either. Their only play is trust.
TAYA
Marcus Sheridan's roofing company generated $14M in revenue by doing one thing: publicly answering every question their customers were afraid to ask. That's the strategy. Your gym client is going to try the same thing — but for fitness.

The client

Iron Ridge Gym — a single-location, owner-operated gym in a college neighborhood. $39/month. No classes, no frills. Free weights, squat racks, good lighting. They're trying to attract first-time gym members who've never walked into a gym and feel intimidated.

The challenge

First-time gym members have a list of fears: What if I look stupid? Do I need to know what I'm doing? Can I actually afford this? Will it be crowded? Will anyone judge me? Iron Ridge can't answer these fears with a discount. They have to answer them with content that tells the truth.

The 4E Framework

Excite
Create desire. Make them want in. Stop the scroll.
Educate
Arm them with answers. Kill the objections before they arise.
Experience
Let them preview before they commit. Reduce the first-visit fear.
Engage
Keep them in the story. Community, progress, conversation.

What you'll do

Step 1 (~10 min): Each person picks one E, and creates a real content piece — a specific title, media type, and trust rationale. Go deep on your one piece.

Step 2 (~3 min): Stop gate — share your piece with your group out loud. Push each other on specificity.

Step 3 (~12 min): As a group, assemble the full 4E content map — one piece per E — then write a Content Trust Statement that ties the whole strategy together.

Engage
Keep people in the story after they join. Build community, celebrate progress, invite conversation.
e.g., A "30 Days In" member spotlight series where new members share what surprised them most

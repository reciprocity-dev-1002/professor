The Scenario

You are part of the leadership team at a new Valvoline Instant Oil Change franchise opening in three weeks. Your team must decide how to design the service experience — from staffing to training to what the customer sees and hears.

But you don't have unlimited resources. Your service design budget is 15 points, and every decision costs something. Max everything = 25 points. You can't have it all.

What You Need to Know

Services are different from products in four fundamental ways:

Intangible
You can't see, touch, or test the service before buying it. The customer can't inspect an oil change before it happens.
Inseparable
The service is produced and consumed at the same time. The technician's behavior IS the product.
Variable
Quality changes depending on who delivers it, when, and how. Different tech = different experience.
Perishable
Services can't be stored. An empty bay at 2pm can't be sold at 5pm. Unused capacity is gone forever.

When designing a service, firms also face service gaps — mismatches between what should happen and what actually happens:

Standards Gap
The difference between what the company intends and what its employees actually do.
Communication Gap
The difference between what you promise in marketing and what you actually deliver.

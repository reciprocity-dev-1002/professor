Your Mission

Your group has been hired as pricing consultants for StreamLocal — a new streaming service featuring local news, local sports, and regional entertainment. You have real cost data, competitor pricing, and customer research.

Your job: set the monthly subscription price using three different pricing methods, then recommend one.

Customer Research

Survey of 1,000 potential subscribers:

  • 60% would pay $5–8/mo
  • 25% would pay $9–12/mo
  • 10% would pay $13–16/mo
  • 5% would pay $17+/mo
  • Most valued: Local sports (78%)
  • Second: Local news (52%)

Round 1: Cost-Plus Pricing

Method Definition

Start with your costs. Add a markup percentage. The price covers costs and delivers a predictable profit.

Price = Variable Cost + (Fixed Cost ÷ Subscribers ÷ 12) + Markup

Each team member: choose a markup, see the price update in real time, and justify your choice.

Round 2: Margin-Based Pricing

Method Definition

Start with your target profit margin. Work backward to find the price that delivers it.

Price = Variable Cost ÷ (1 - Target Margin%)

Note: This method ignores fixed costs in the per-unit calculation. You need enough volume to cover fixed costs separately.

Each team member: choose a target margin, see the price, and justify your choice.

Round 3: Value-Based Pricing

Method Definition

Start with what customers are willing to pay. Price to the value they perceive, not what it costs you.

What Customers Will Pay

  • 60% → $5–8/mo
  • 25% → $9–12/mo
  • 10% → $13–16/mo
  • 5% → $17+/mo

Most Valued Features

  • Local sports: 78% value it
  • Local news: 52% value it

Each team member: pick a price, then explain which customer segment you're targeting and why.

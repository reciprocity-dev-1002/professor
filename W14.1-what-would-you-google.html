The Case
The Story
$14M in revenue. Zero cold calls.
A roofing company in the mid-2000s was hemorrhaging customers during the financial crisis. Instead of cutting prices or hiring more salespeople, they did something their competitors refused to do: they answered every question their customers were afraid to ask.

What they did

They published blog posts answering the exact questions homeowners were Googling. Pricing. Timelines. Comparisons. Even "how to spot a bad contractor." Within three years, their website was the most-visited roofing site in the world. They closed $14 million in sales — mostly from people who found their content before ever talking to a salesperson.

Your job today: figure out which questions they had to answer, what content they built — and why it worked.

The 4E Framework — your classification tool

Excite
Create buzz. Make content relevant and attention-grabbing at the exact right moment.
Educate
Inform. Remove confusion and fear by giving customers the knowledge they need.
Experience
Let customers mentally rehearse what it's like to work with you — before they commit.
Engage
Build an ongoing relationship. Not just one article — a reason to keep coming back.

How this works

Block 1 (~8 min): Classify 6 questions customers actually Google. For each: pick the E it maps to, pick the best content type, and write one sentence explaining why answering it builds trust.

Stop gate: Compare your E classifications with your group. You'll need to tell us what you disagreed on before you can continue.

Block 2 (~5 min): Classify 4 pieces of content the company built in response. Same process — E, content type, trust rationale.

Group decision (~5 min): Pick the 2 pieces of content the company should prioritize first — and write your shared rationale.

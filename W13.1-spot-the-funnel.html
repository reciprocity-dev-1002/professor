Week 13 · Exercise 1
How This Works
Netflix spent the biggest brand-partnership budget of the decade on Stranger Things' final season. Your job is to figure out whether it worked — and how they'd even know.

The one action Netflix actually wants

Every one of these activations exists to do one thing: get someone to watch Stranger Things. Not "feel warm about Netflix." Not "remember the show." Watch it. That's the only Action in Netflix's AIDA funnel.

So for each activation, the real junior-marketer question is: how would you prove a single view came from this? Sometimes you can. Sometimes you get a proxy. Sometimes you're guessing with a spreadsheet.

Your two frameworks today

The AIDA Funnel
Attention · did they notice?
Interest · did they lean in?
Desire · did they want it?
Action · did they watch?
Measurement Ladder
Trackable · clean data
Proxy · indirect signal
Unknowable · brand vibes
What do you report Monday?

The IMC tool is given to you at the top of each round. Your job isn't to guess what tool it is — it's to decide where in the funnel it pushes, and whether Netflix can actually prove it worked.
